Fire prevention month is observed in March to raise awareness about the importance of fire safety measures. During this month, people are encouraged to take steps to prevent fires from happening, such as properly storing flammable materials and regularly inspecting heating appliances. Fire drills and educational programs are also organized to educate individuals and communities about fire safety. This month serves as a reminder for everyone to be proactive in preventing fires and ensuring the safety of their homes and workplaces.

To do's when a fire occurs

If possible, close doors and windows to slow down the spread of the fire. If there is smoke, stay low to the ground where the air is less toxic. If your clothing catches on fire, stop, drop, and roll to smother the flames. Call 911 or your local emergency number as soon as you are safely outside.

representation Ways to prevent fires
Keep flammable materials away from heat sources: This includes items such as curtains, bedding, and paper that can easily catch fire. Keep these items away from heaters, stoves, and other heat sources.
Install and maintain smoke detectors: Smoke detectors can alert you to a fire before it gets out of control.
Don't leave the stove unattended,Cooking is one of the leading causes of house fires. Always stay in the kitchen when cooking, and never leave the stove or oven unattended
Properly store flammable liquids: Gasoline, cleaning fluids, and other flammable liquids should be stored in a cool, dry place away from heat sources. Make sure the containers are tightly sealed.
Create a plan for how to escape your home in case of a fire, and make sure everyone in your household knows what to do.
